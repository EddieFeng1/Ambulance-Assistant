  NHS is under huge pressure

'The number of ambulance emergency calls that received a face-to-face response grew steadily between 2011-12 and 2016-17 by 2.9% per year on average from 6.1 million to 6.9 million. In 2017, the way ambulance incidents were recorded changed making direct comparison to previous years difficult. Between 2018-19 and 2019-20, the number of incidents increased by 4.6% from 8.4 million to 8.8 million. In 2020-21, the year of the pandemic, the number of incidents fell by 1.5% to 8.7 million before increasing by 2.6% to 8.9 million in 2021-22'[1].

 Importance of fast ambulance for patients

During emergencies, prehospital medical care and patient transport are successfully provided by ambulance and paramedic services. It is possible to reduce the severity of the damages inflicted on the patient’s health due to ambulance services. Effective patient assessment and transfer are greatly facilitated in dangerous and stressful circumstances thanks to ambulance equipment and the paramedics’ capabilities. Paramedics can perform all of the services that different medical professionals can individually and separately provide. With regular communication between ambulances, hospitals, and emergency doctors or medical personnel, it is possible to guarantee that patients receive the best care, advice, and treatment in the fastest way possible.

 What situations can be helped?

This website aims to help those patients who need help at remote places located in the closet hospital as soon as possible. On the other hand, this website could provide navigation for ambulance drivers, helping them get hospital soon. Imagine that you are travelling to a new place where you've never been before, enjoying the fantastic natural views. But suddenly, you fell because focusing so much on the nice landscape and cracked your arm. In this situation, prompt first care is most important. The functions of the website could help you find the closest hospital and get there as soon as possible. Saving time not only helps you to receive treatment but also gives other people who may need ambulance service next.
